Food Inflation Market Intelligence Insights

- Key staples such as rice, sugar, and vegetable oil are the main inflation drivers in Somali urban markets.
- Monthly price growth for core food items averages 3% to 5% in USD terms and 4% to 6% in SOS terms, indicating currency pressure is contributing to local food inflation.
- Regional disparities persist: fuel-dependent cities like Bosaso show higher food inflation volatility, while central markets like Baydhabo remain more stable.
- Recommendations: target supply chain interventions around high-risk products, improve market monitoring for retail and wholesale spreads, and use food inflation indices to support procurement timing for humanitarian and commercial buyers.
